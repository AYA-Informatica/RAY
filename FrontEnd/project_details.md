
RAY
The Infrastructure of Local Commerce in East Africa
1. The Core Idea (What RAY Really Is)
RAY is not just a classifieds app.
It is a behavior engine.
At its core, RAY exists to replace this current behavior in Rwanda:
“Let me post this on Facebook and hope someone sees it.”
With a new behavior:
“Let me list this on RAY — someone nearby will find it fast.”
That shift is everything.
Platforms like OLX and Jiji succeeded because they didn’t just provide listings — they
organized chaos.
RAY goes one step further:
● It organizes
● It builds trust
● It accelerates deals
2. The Problem You Are Solving (Brutally Honest)
Right now in Rwanda, buying and selling looks like this:
● Facebook groups are messy, unstructured, full of duplicates and scams
● No filtering, no real search intelligence
● Trust is weak — anyone can post anything
● Communication is fragmented (comments, DMs, WhatsApp)
● Serious sellers are mixed with unserious users
The result:
● Buyers waste time
● Sellers don’t get serious leads
● High-value items (cars, property) feel risky
RAY fixes this by doing something simple but powerful:
It turns informal trade into structured, trusted, fast-moving local commerce.
3. The Product Experience (How RAY Feels)
RAY must feel like this:
● Fast — actions happen instantly
● Local — everything feels “near me”
● Safe — users feel protected without friction
● Simple — no learning curve
If a first-time user needs instructions, you’ve already failed.
4. The User Flow (This Is Where Products Win or Die)
4.1 Onboarding — The First 30 Seconds
The onboarding must feel almost invisible.
User opens app → enters phone number → OTP → done.
No email. No long forms.
Immediately after login:
● The app asks:
“What are you looking for today?”
This is not a form. It’s a psychological hook.
Options appear:
● Phones
● Cars
● Houses
● Furniture
● Jobs
● “Browse everything”
The goal is to activate intent immediately.
4.2 Selling Flow — The Heart of RAY
Selling must feel like sending a message, not filling a form.
User taps “Sell”
Instead of a long form, RAY guides step-by-step:
1. “What are you selling?”
→ Smart category suggestions (AI-assisted typing)
2. “Add photos”
→ Camera opens instantly
→ System nudges:
“Items with 3+ photos sell 4x faster”
3. “Set your price”
→ RAY suggests a price range based on similar listings
4. “Add a short description”
→ Optional, not forced
5. Location auto-detected (GPS)
6. Post
From tap → live listing should take under 60 seconds
That’s your benchmark.
4.3 Buying Flow — Where Retention Happens
Buyer opens app → immediately sees:
● Listings near them
● Clean, visual grid
● Price + location visible instantly
Search must feel intelligent:
● “iPhone 11 Kigali” should just work
Filters must be simple:
● Price range
● Distance
● Condition
When user taps a listing:
They should feel clarity in 2 seconds:
● What it is
● How much
● Where it is
● Who is selling
Then one dominant action:
“Chat with seller”
4.4 Chat System — The Engine of Transactions
This is not just messaging.
This is where deals happen.
Chat must include:
● Quick replies (“Is it still available?”)
● Option to share phone number
● Call button (optional unlock)
Important psychological detail:
Users must feel:
“I am in control of who can reach me.”
4.5 Closing the Deal
RAY does NOT handle payments initially.
This is critical.
Why?
Because forcing payments early kills trust in emerging markets.
Instead:
● Users meet physically
● Or use mobile money (outside platform)
RAY’s role is:
Connect → Enable → Protect
Not control.
5. Trust System (This Will Decide If You Win or Lose)
Africa marketplaces fail or succeed on trust.
RAY must build layered trust, not forced verification.
Level 1: Soft Trust
● Phone number verification
● Account age indicator
● “Active user” badge
Level 2: Behavioral Trust
● Fast replies
● Completed deals
● Positive interactions
Level 3: Strong Trust (Optional Paid/Verified)
● Verified seller badge
● ID verification
● Dealer status
The key insight:
Trust should grow naturally, not be forced at signup.
6. Monetization (How RAY Makes Real Money)
Never charge users at the beginning.
Growth first. Always.
Later, introduce:
6.1 Visibility Economy (Core Revenue)
Exactly like OLX, but improved:
Sellers pay to:
● Boost listings
● Appear at top
● Get more visibility
But RAY must communicate it like this:
“Get more buyers faster”
Not:
“Pay us”
6.2 Premium Seller System (Your Biggest Revenue Driver)
Inspired by OLX Elite, but localized:
Features:
● “Top Seller” badge
● Priority placement
● Direct call unlock
● More listings allowed
Target users:
● Phone resellers
● Car dealers
● Property agents
These users will pay consistently.
6.3 Business Accounts
For serious sellers:
● Dashboard
● Analytics (views, chats)
● Branding
This turns RAY into a B2B tool, not just C2C.
6.4 Advertising (Later Stage)
Once traffic grows:
● Banner ads
● Sponsored listings
High margin, low effort.
7. The Strategic Advantage (Why RAY Can Win)
RAY’s advantage is not technology.
It is focus + localization.
While global platforms think broadly, RAY thinks:
● Kigali first
● Kinyarwanda support
● Mobile money culture
● Local trust dynamics
And most importantly:
RAY is built for how Africans actually trade — not how Silicon Valley thinks they
should.
8. Go-To-Market Strategy (How You Actually Win)
You don’t launch nationwide.
You dominate Kigali first.
Phase 1:
● Focus on:
○ Phones
○ Furniture
○ Rentals
These categories move fast.
Phase 2:
● Add cars, jobs, services
Phase 3:
● Expand to other cities
Growth tactics:
● WhatsApp sharing of listings
● Referral incentives
● Early “verified sellers”
9. The Psychological Layer (The Hidden Advantage)
If you get this right, RAY becomes addictive.
Users must feel:
● “I might find a good deal if I check now”
● “Someone might message me soon”
● “I don’t want to miss out”
That’s how platforms grow daily usage.
10. The Long-Term Vision
RAY starts as classifieds.
But it evolves into:
● The default place to buy/sell anything locally
● A business tool for sellers
● A trusted commerce layer across East Africa
Eventually, you can add:
● Delivery
● Escrow
● In-app payments
But only after trust is established.
Final Truth
If you execute this correctly:
RAY will not just compete with Facebook Marketplace.
It will replace it — locally, gradually, then completely.
